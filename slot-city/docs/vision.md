# Slot City — Vision

## What Is Slot City?

Slot City is a browser-based multiplayer social casino world built on virtual (fictional) in-game chips. There is no real money, no real gambling, and no connection to any regulated gambling system. It is a social entertainment product.

Players create accounts, enter an isometric casino lobby, walk their avatars around in real time, chat with other players, play poker, join tournaments, and hang out in a bar/lounge.

## The Feeling We're Building

Slot City should feel like a hybrid of:

- **The Sims Online** — social, avatar-driven, emergent gameplay through interaction
- **Habbo Hotel** — isometric rooms, furniture, social gathering spots
- **Club Penguin** — approachable, safe, community-first, expressive characters
- **PokerStars** — serious poker mechanics with clear game flow
- **Retro neon Vegas** — visual energy, warmth, excitement, glamour

The game should feel like a **social venue**, not just a menu of casino minigames. The casino itself is the content.

## Core Player Loop

1. Create account
2. Enter isometric casino lobby
3. Move avatar in real time
4. See other players, chat, emote
5. Visit poker room or bar/lounge
6. Sit at tables, join tournaments
7. Earn and spend chips
8. Unlock identity through cosmetics and progression

## World Structure

The casino is organized as a set of connected isometric rooms. Each room is a social space with its own personality and purpose.

| Room | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Lobby / Entrance** | Arrival hub, navigation, tournament announcement board |
| **Poker Room** | Seated multiplayer Texas Hold'em, spectator seating |
| **Bar / Lounge** | Social heart of the casino — chat, watch tournaments, relax |
| **Slot Floor** | Rows of slot machines (Phase 6) |
| **Blackjack Corner** | Dealer-based card game (Phase 2+) |
| **VIP Area** | Exclusive high-stakes tables (Phase 6) |
| **Tournament Hall** | Bracket display, live multi-table events (Phase 4) |

Rooms are connected by walk-up portals. Players physically walk their avatar to a door to change rooms.

## Why This Works

Social casual games succeed when players have reasons to be in the same place at the same time and things to do together. Slot City creates this by:

- **Shared physical space**: Everyone moves around the same isometric casino
- **Real games**: Poker is real Texas Hold'em, not fake UI
- **Ambient socialization**: Bar/lounge as a social heart between sessions
- **Tournaments**: Structured competitive events that give rhythm and reason to return
- **Identity**: Avatars and chips let players project status and personality
- **Progression**: Cosmetics, VIP levels, and achievements reward loyalty

## Character Vision

Avatars are simple Sims-like isometric humans with:

- Adult proportions (not chibi)
- Readable silhouettes in crowded social environments
- Low-detail but distinct clothing
- Limited animation frames (idle, walk, seated, emote)
- Easy recolor system for cosmetics (single outfit tint value)
- Username label always visible above the head

The character style deliberately avoids highly realistic anatomy, complex 3D rigs, or animation pipelines that require heavy toolchains. The goal is fast iteration, easy AI-generated asset creation, and a social readability that works in dense rooms.

## What It Is Not

- **Not real-money gambling** — all currency is fictional, the product is social entertainment
- **Not a 3D game** — the browser-based isometric 2.5D perspective keeps it accessible and fast
- **Not a native app** — runs entirely in the browser with no installation required
- **Not a menu of disconnected minigames** — the casino world is persistent and social
- **Not a shallow prototype** — server-authoritative architecture and real game logic from day one

## Tone

*The Sims Online meets neon Vegas in an isometric browser casino.*

Warm, neon-lit, social, slightly glamorous — the energy of a late-night Vegas casino floor without the stress of real stakes. Players should feel like they're hanging out in a cool place, not grinding a reward system.
